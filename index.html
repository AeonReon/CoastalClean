Before they even unpack, they've already formed an opinion. And cleanliness is the single biggest factor in whether they leave you 5 stars or 4.

The Hidden Problem

Turnover Cleans Keep Things Tidy.
But They Can't Keep Things Fresh.

Even with a great turnover after every guest, grime builds up in places nobody reaches during a quick changeover. That's when the "it looks clean but smells a bit off" reviews start appearing.

Airbnb themselves recommend a deep clean every 2–3 months for active properties. High-turnover listings on the North Coast — especially during summer — need it even more often.

Without regular deep cleans, this builds up invisibly:

  • Inside the oven & microwave — grease and food splatter guests always check
  • Behind the fridge & under furniture — dust balls that cause stale odours
  • Grout, tile seals & shower screens — mould and limescale that screams "neglected"
  • Mattress, pillows & upholstery — sweat, allergens, and that "not quite fresh" feeling
  • Window tracks & skirting boards — the first thing detail-oriented guests spot
